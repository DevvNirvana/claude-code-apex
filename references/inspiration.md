## What to Study on Each Site

When analyzing an Awwwards site or Dribbble shot:

### 1. First impression (1 second)
What is the dominant visual element? Color, type, image, or animation?
This tells you what they prioritized in the hierarchy.

### 2. Type scale
What's the ratio between h1 and body text? Award-winning sites typically use a 3:1 to 5:1 ratio.
What font pairing? Serif heading + sans body is almost always editorial.

### 3. Color restraint
How many colors are used? Award-winning sites almost always use 2-3 colors max.
Where is the accent color applied? Almost always: one CTA, one highlight.

### 4. Motion quality
What triggers animations? Scroll, hover, or mount?
Are they meaningful (state change) or decorative (background)?
How long? Sub-400ms for interactions, 400-800ms for entrance animations.

### 5. Whitespace
What is the smallest gap between any two elements? That tells you their spacing unit.
What is the largest space? That tells you their section separation distance.

### 6. The "unexpected delight"
What is the ONE thing on this page that makes you stop and smile?
This is usually the React Bits equivalent — one special animated element.

## The Awwwards Design Formula

Sites that consistently win or get honorable mentions share these traits:

1. **One exceptional typographic moment** — usually the hero headline
2. **One unexpected interactive element** — cursor effect, scroll animation, 3D
3. **Extreme whitespace confidence** — more padding than seems "safe"
4. **Color commitment** — one strong color applied with discipline
5. **Consistent proportions** — same spacing scale used everywhere
6. **Smooth, purposeful motion** — not flashy, but intentional
7. **Photography/illustration quality** — the visual assets are exceptional

## Practical Research Workflow

### For any new design request:
1. Identify product category (SaaS, e-commerce, creative, etc.)
2. Visit Lapa.ninja → filter by category → spend 10 minutes studying 3-5 examples
3. Note: what spacing scale, what typography weight, what color count
4. Visit SiteInspire → filter by style → find a "feel" match
5. Check Godly.website → look at transition animations in the same category
6. Now design — with references in mind, not copied

## Color Trends by Year (for reference)

### 2024-2025 dominant trends:
- **AI Purple** — violet-to-indigo gradient for anything AI-powered
- **Dark Premium** — near-black with subtle color accents (not true black)
- **Warm Minimal** — cream/off-white backgrounds with warm browns/oranges
- **Glassmorphism matured** — less frosted excess, more restrained glass cards
- **Organic gradients** — aurora/mesh gradients replacing flat colors

### What's receding:
- Neumorphism (impractical, low contrast)
- Heavy glassmorphism everywhere
- Neon on black (unless gaming)
- Bright gradients as primary backgrounds (unless justified by brand)

### Typography trends 2024-2025:
- **Variable fonts** going mainstream (responsive weight on scroll/hover)
- **Display serifs** making a comeback for editorial sections
- **Geometric grotesque** (Space Grotesk, Plus Jakarta, Outfit) replacing plain sans
- **Mono fonts** used intentionally for technical/code aesthetics (not decorative)
